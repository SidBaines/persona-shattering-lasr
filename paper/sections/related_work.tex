\textbf{Personas and personality in LLMs.}
\citet{lu2026assistant} identify an `assistant axis' in activation space as a strong predictor of model behaviour, and \citet{marks2026persona} propose the Persona Selection Model to explain how LLMs select among personas. These works study personas via activations; we complement them with weight-space interventions that persist across contexts.

\textbf{Character and trait training.}
\citet{maiya2025opencharacter} demonstrate that LoRA fine-tuning with constitutional prompts can induce specific character archetypes. We build on their pipeline but focus on psychometrically grounded OCEAN traits rather than ad hoc character descriptions, and study the composability and geometry of the resulting adapters.

\textbf{Psychometric evaluation of LLMs.}
\citet{suh2024rediscovering} show that LLMs can act as trait descriptors, rediscovering latent personality dimensions. The TRAIT benchmark \citep{lee2024trait} provides MCQ-based personality measurement for LLMs. We use TRAIT alongside calibrated LLM judges to evaluate trait transfer.

\textbf{Task arithmetic and model composition.}
\citet{ilharco2023editing} show that weight-space directions encode task-specific knowledge and can be composed additively. \citet{huang2024chat} demonstrate a similar principle for transferring instruction-following across languages via chat vectors. We extend this paradigm to personality traits, demonstrating that LoRA adapters for different OCEAN dimensions can be linearly combined.

\textbf{Activation-space personality steering.}
\citet{turner2024activation} explore steering model behaviour via activation additions. We compare our weight-space approach to activation capping \citep{lu2026assistant} and find improved coherence and robustness at equivalent trait expression levels.
